## 1. Market Environment & Fundamentals

The USD/JPY pair faces a fundamentally supportive environment driven by multiple concurrent catalysts. Federal Reserve Chair Powell's announced departure from the central bank marks the conclusion of one of the most volatile policy eras in modern Federal Reserve history, injecting significant uncertainty into the monetary policy trajectory. This leadership transition, combined with persistent inflation fears, has intensified market volatility and driven Treasury yields higher across the curve. The 10-year yield's ascension directly narrows the yield differential advantage that had been eroding the dollar's carry advantage against the yen, now reversing that dynamic decisively in favor of the greenback.

Simultaneously, risk-off sentiment has reasserted itself across global markets. US major indices closed markedly lower, with today's declines erasing the entirety of the week's gains—a development that traditionally would support the Japanese yen as a safe-haven currency. However, the confluence of elevated yields and a firmer dollar has overridden the typical risk-aversion bid for the yen. Commodity markets reflect this dynamic: gold has tumbled sharply, pressured by both the stronger dollar and higher real yields, reinforcing the broad-based dollar strength narrative. Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East—specifically Israel's targeted strike against Hamas leadership in Gaza—add an additional layer of macro uncertainty that further supports dollar-denominated assets as the primary global reserve currency.

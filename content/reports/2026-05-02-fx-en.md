## 1. Market Environment & Fundamentals

The fundamental backdrop for USD/JPY reflects a distinctly risk-on environment driven by multiple catalysts. The sixth consecutive week of US equity gains demonstrates sustained investor confidence in the American economic outlook, historically a supportive factor for the dollar-yen pair given the currency's sensitivity to global risk appetite. This equity momentum suggests capital flows continuing to favor dollar-denominated assets.

Simultaneously, President Trump's announcement of increased tariffs on European automobiles introduces a new vector of dollar strength. Trade policy tightening typically reinforces safe-haven demand for the USD while simultaneously pressuring European currencies—the euro's weakness indirectly strengthens the yen cross through EUR/JPY dynamics, but the primary translation remains dollar dominance. This protectionist measure signals continued US trade leverage, a theme that has consistently underpinned dollar resilience in recent quarters.

On the geopolitical front, the formal notification to Congress that US hostilities with Tehran have concluded removes a significant tail-risk premium from markets. While Trump's expression of dissatisfaction with Iran's latest sanctions relief proposal introduces uncertainty regarding ultimate nuclear deal prospects, the immediate de-escalation reduces flight-to-safety flows that typically benefit the yen. The combination of equity strength, tariff-driven dollar reinforcement, and diminished geopolitical risk premium establishes a fundamentally bullish case for USD/JPY near-term.

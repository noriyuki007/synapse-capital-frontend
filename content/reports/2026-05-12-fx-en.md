## 1. Market Environment & Fundamentals

Two major developments are shaping the USD/JPY outlook. First, Iran's announcement that it is prepared to dilute its highly enriched uranium to levels of 3.7% and 20% signals a significant de-escalation of geopolitical tensions. This reduces the demand for traditional safe-haven assets like the Japanese yen, thereby capping JPY strength. Second, the US Treasury's auction of $58 billion in three-year notes at a high yield of 3.965% underscores the market's expectation of persistently elevated interest rates. The resulting yield advantage continues to support the US dollar against low-yielding counterparts, including the yen.

These forces are counterbalanced by lingering risks. Reports that former President Trump will meet with his national security team to discuss the Iran conflict reintroduce uncertainty, which could temporarily boost JPY demand. Additionally, USD weakness against the Swiss franc and Canadian dollar—evidenced by USDCHF hitting new day lows and USDCAD declining—suggests some broad-based dollar selling. However, these moves appear more reflective of idiosyncratic factors: the CHF's safe-haven bid amid unrelated risk aversion, and the CAD's sensitivity to crude oil fluctuations. Consequently, the net effect leans bullish for USD/JPY, as the yield differential and improving risk appetite outweigh the sporadic risk-off impulses.

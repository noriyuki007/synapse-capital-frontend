The fundamental backdrop for USD/JPY remains decisively dollar-bullish as we enter the week of May 18-22. The convergence of silver and gold lower amid surging real yields signals market repricing of Federal Reserve tightening expectations—a development that directly favors the greenback against its Japanese counterpart. Higher real yields in the United States relative to Japan's ultra-accommodative policy stance continue to widen the rate differential, a structural driver that has proven persistent throughout 2025-2026.

The energy complex adds an additional layer of yen weakness. IEA's warning regarding rapidly depleting commercial oil inventories suggests supply-side constraints remain a material risk. As a net energy importer, Japan faces deteriorating terms of trade when oil prices appreciate—a dynamic that historically weighs on the yen. The prolonged US-Iran stalemate compounds this risk, maintaining geopolitical premium on crude. Meanwhile, Bank of England's Greene's admonition against looking through negative supply shocks reflects a broader central bank consensus: supply-side inflation is no longer to be dismissed as transitory. This macro environment—where central banks prioritize fighting inflation over growth concerns—continues to support carry trades and currency pairs where rate differentials favor the higher-yielding currency.
